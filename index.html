Our Goal

Crimson Rocketry is a place for future engineers to explore a wide variety of aerospace concepts by designing, building, and launching high-power Rockets. Collaborating with local rocketry organizations, we safely push the boundaries of high-school rocketry in Minnesota.

National Association of Rocketry

On 2/22/20 we organized a meeting with the National Association of Rocketry to learn about how NAR and Tripoli work in Minnesota. During this visit, we got to learn launch procedures and generally just absorb as much information as possible. We also launched a few rockets and learned a lot about the procedures.

Apollo 11

Apollo 11 was the first manned mission to land on the Moon. The first steps by humans on another planetary body were taken by Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin on July 20, 1969. The astronauts also returned to Earth the first samples from another planetary body.

Voyager 1

Voyager 1 is a space probe that was launched by NASA on September 5, 1977. Part of the Voyager program to study the outer Solar System, Voyager 1 was launched 16 days after its twin, Voyager 2.

Crimson Rocketry Club COVID Safety Plan

Pat Mulcahy 763-391-8700 - MGSH Teacher/Rocketry Club Instructor


  • Masks will be required to be worn by particpants any time they are in a meeting
  • Instructor will be required to wear a mask for the entirety of the meeting
  • Instructor will be responsible for disinfecting all used equipment after each session
  • Pods of no more than 30 participants including coaches will be allowed per meeting at the same time and distancing as per district guidlines will be followed
  • Instructor will record attendance at the beginning of each practice session (for tracing purposes)
  • Participants will be asked to check themselves daily for symptoms before attending a meeting
  • Participants will not be allowed to participate if they are not feeling well
  • Participants will be sent home at the first sign of not feeling well or having a temperature over 100 (CDC guidlines)
  • If any participant were to start feeling ill during the session, that participant will be sent home with a request for them to get tested. The remainder of the session will be canceled for all participants and any participant who has been in close contact with the impacted participant (within 6 feet for more than 15 minutes) would also be recommended to get tested
  • There will be hand sanitizer available for participants/coaches to use before and after sessions
